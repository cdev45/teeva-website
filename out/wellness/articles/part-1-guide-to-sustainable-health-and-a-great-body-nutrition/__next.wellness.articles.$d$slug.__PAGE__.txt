<h1>Guide to sustainable health and a great body, Part 1, Nutrition</h1><p>Do you want to lose weight, or simply look great in-and-out of your clothing? Achieving your health goals in a sustainable and long-term objective is more about balance than it is about extreme dieting. While extreme regimens can net you expedient results, they are commonly difficult to sustain, and therefore have a high abandon rate. Alternatively, adopting a balanced program offers you a gradual acclimation experience where you are in full control of the process; all for the benefit of achieving and sustaining your weight goal for life.</p><table><tbody><tr><td colspan="1" rowspan="1"><p><img alt="Photo by Trang Doan: https://www.pexels.com/photo/variety-of-food-on-wooden-coaster-793759/" src="/assets/articles/part-1-guide-to-sustainable-health-and-a-great-body-nutrition/image1.jpg" title="" class="article-img"></p></td></tr><tr><td colspan="1" rowspan="1"><p>Photo by <a href="https://www.google.com/url?q=https://www.blogger.com/blog/post/edit/6575825020937678241/175977338254176457%23&amp;sa=D&amp;source=editors&amp;ust=1782306156752999&amp;usg=AOvVaw3JnMy63Pe3iLbm_A9PLBsw">Trang Doan</a></p></td></tr></tbody></table><h2>Nutrition</h2><p>A calorie is not just a calorie. Similar to fuel for a Sports Car, you want to supply your body with <a href="https://www.google.com/url?q=https://www.fda.gov/regulatory-information/search-fda-guidance-documents/guidance-industry-use-term-healthy-labeling-human-food-products&amp;sa=D&amp;source=editors&amp;ust=1782306156753423&amp;usg=AOvVaw3ytwdNdLYR1oB_Oo9bMrmN">nourishing fuel</a>&nbsp;that will yield optimal performance.</p><p>Quality In --&gt; Quality Out, &nbsp;Garbage In --&gt; Garbage Out</p><p>The cleaner the food you ingest the better. Processed food is exactly what it sounds – manipulated. The “purer” the food you ingest, the greater nutrition acquired. For this purpose, we recommend focusing your efforts on <a href="https://www.google.com/url?q=https://www.ewg.org/&amp;sa=D&amp;source=editors&amp;ust=1782306156753859&amp;usg=AOvVaw0yi_fRP7WIo3yQqlbJPt4f">Organic</a>&nbsp;and <a href="https://www.google.com/url?q=https://www.nongmoproject.org/&amp;sa=D&amp;source=editors&amp;ust=1782306156753927&amp;usg=AOvVaw10JChPaRrKpaNX-0dCZdLq">Non-GMO foods</a>. A study of 68,946 adults published in <a href="https://www.google.com/url?q=https://jamanetwork.com/journals/jamainternalmedicine/article-abstract/2707948&amp;sa=D&amp;source=editors&amp;ust=1782306156754069&amp;usg=AOvVaw3RxN6yXuE0cLBBgWLaVcys">The Journal of the American Medical Association</a>&nbsp;found people who frequently eat organic foods lowered their overall risk of developing cancer. Strive to construct the majority of your meals of Organic and Non-GMO foods whenever possible. Your “Strict” days should be entirely comprised of Organic and Non-GMO foods. Whereas your “Indulgent” days are flexible. This will provide a well-balanced schedule, delivering copious nutrition while still allowing for personal indulgences.</p><ul><li>Fruits: Go crazy!</li></ul><ul><li>Blueberries</li><li>Blackberries</li><li>Strawberries</li><li>Watermelon</li><li>Grapes</li><li>Pretty much every fruit…</li></ul><ul><li>Vegetables: Go crazy!</li></ul><ul><li>Spinach</li><li>Kale</li><li>Broccoli</li><li>Peas</li><li>Tomatoes</li><li>Carrots</li><li>Pretty much every vegetable…</li></ul><ul><li>Protein</li></ul><ul><li>Eggs</li><li>Fish</li><li>Lean Meats: Turkey, Chicken</li><li>Peanut Butter</li></ul><ul><li>Dairy</li></ul><ul><li>Milk: Almond Milk or Hormone-free Cow Milk</li><li>Cheese: Mozzarella or Hard Cheeses</li><li>Yogurt</li></ul><ul><li>Oats and Grains</li></ul><ul><li>Oatmeal: Steel Cut or Rolled Oats</li><li>Cereals: Low Sugar and Fortified Vitamins</li><li>Bread: Whole Grain</li><li>Rice: Long grain Rice</li></ul><ul><li>Misc Foods</li></ul><ul><li>Nuts: Almonds, Cashews, etc.</li><li>Beans</li><li>Olives</li><li>Oils: Olive, Canola</li></ul><h2>Calorie Surplus and Deficit</h2><p>Calories serve as fuel for the body’s daily functional activities. As in all things, too much or too little of something is unfavorable. The objective with your daily calorie intake is to ensure you are consuming only what your body needs, and nothing more. A surplus occurs when your body receives an excess amount of calories, and a deficit occurs when your body is not supplied a sufficient amount. </p><h4>Example: Calorie Surplus and Deficit over 14 days</h4><p><img alt="" src="/assets/articles/part-1-guide-to-sustainable-health-and-a-great-body-nutrition/image3.png" title="" class="article-img"></p><p>Important: This chart is to serve as an example only. <h1>Guide to sustainable health and a great body, Part 2, Exercise</h1><p>Do you want to build muscle, reduce fat, or simply increase tone? Possessing health and a body that functions to its potential is entirely accomplishable and is more about balance than it is about extremes. Extreme fitness programs can net you expedient results, but are commonly difficult to sustain. Everything in nature teaches us about balance. By transferring this lesson into an effective health program, we yield a confluence of both science and nature; producing a sustainable and functional healthy body for years of enjoyment.</p><table><tbody><tr><td colspan="1" rowspan="1"><p><img alt="Photo by Kamaji Ogino: https://www.pexels.com/photo/young-asian-woman-piggybacking-smiling-daughter-while-exercising-at-home-5094677/" src="/assets/articles/part-2-guide-to-sustainable-health-and-a-great-body-exercise/image1.jpg" title="" class="article-img"></p></td></tr><tr><td colspan="1" rowspan="1"><p>Photo by <a href="https://www.google.com/url?q=https://www.blogger.com/blog/post/edit/6575825020937678241/8536049974643861661%23&amp;sa=D&amp;source=editors&amp;ust=1782306165600571&amp;usg=AOvVaw0pmccC6nQ50vbmR_t_V71e">Kamaji Ogino</a></p></td></tr></tbody></table><h2>Exercise</h2><p>The importance of exercise <a href="https://www.google.com/url?q=https://www.blogger.com/blog/post/edit/6575825020937678241/8536049974643861661%23&amp;sa=D&amp;source=editors&amp;ust=1782306165601137&amp;usg=AOvVaw1rJBpUAGhwtw-roEfcAJgu">combined with nutrition</a>&nbsp;cannot be understated. They are Yin &amp; Yang in Life. They are mutually inclusive; as in one is not fully effective without the other. It is critical to incorporate exercise into your daily schedule. I have found that scheduling exercise daily not only <a href="https://www.google.com/url?q=https://www.blogger.com/blog/post/edit/6575825020937678241/8536049974643861661%23&amp;sa=D&amp;source=editors&amp;ust=1782306165601754&amp;usg=AOvVaw0HSMUGhL9CQyNpw7XE-Wgi">produces the greatest results</a>, it is also tolerant of unexpected inconveniences. For example, should life prevent you from exercising on a particular day, the program and therefore your results will not suffer.</p><p>Here is important terminology, rules, and information for you to apply to the fitness programs below.</p><ul><li>Terms</li></ul><ul><li>Rep = a single full contraction of the engaged muscle group.</li><li>Set = a quantity of Reps to be performed succinctly.</li></ul><ul><li>Training Methods</li></ul><ul><li>Station Training: Defines the more traditional style of training whereby you complete all sets of a particular exercise in sequence.</li><li>Circuit Training: By alternating between exercises, you provide the engaged muscle groups the opportunity to rest between Sets. (Ex: Alternate between Leg Press and Chest Press) A common benefit of circuit training is the reduction of time within the gym.</li></ul><ul><li>Resistance Training Rules</li></ul><ul><li>Control: All exercises should be performed with correct form, slow &amp; steady control = 2 count Up ... 2 count Down.</li><li>Increase: You should barely be able to perform final repetition for each exercise listed. If you can perform all repetitions of the final Set, increase the weight by 5lbs. Every increase in weight will result in the loss of 2 Reps.</li><li>Post-Workout Stretch: Ensure to provide adequate time to stretch your entire body at the conclusion of your exercise.</li></ul><h2>Weekly Exercise Schedules</h2><p>Below you will discover prescribed exercise programs designed to accomplish two common fitness objectives: Functional fitness and Athleticism. Instead our objective is to develop a responsive well-defined body that will elevate our lives for years, allow for greater life achievements and experiences while also looking fantastic in-and-out of clothing. Depending on your current aspirations, you may decide to elect one over the other. Here’s a summary to help you navigate:</p><ul><li>Program A: This program focuses on natural body weight. By training your body to move its own frame through various positions, you will develop speed, agility, flexibility and a toned muscle physique.</li></ul><ul><li>Program B: This program is a hybrid between body weight and greater resistance.
